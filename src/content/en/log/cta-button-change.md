### 1. Spotting the Problem (Data)

Looking at data from about 100 recent visitors, people were spending nearly **2 minutes on average** carefully reading through the landing page.

But there were **zero clicks**.

They're reading. But no one is pressing the button.

The numbers were speaking: "Something's wrong with this page."

### 2. Forming a Hypothesis

Why weren't people clicking?

The most prominent button said **"Start for Free"** â€” but I concluded that visitors were hesitating because clicking it felt like it would lead to something complicated: **psychological friction** and **uncertainty about the outcome**.

Even with "free" right there, the word "start" feels heavy somehow.

> *"Wait, does clicking this mean I'm signing up for something?"*
> *"Start what exactly? Do I have to do something?"*

The moment a question mark appears in a visitor's head, their finger stops.

### 3. What We Did (Action)

Instead of rebuilding the whole system, we edited the **smallest unit of customer contact: the CTA copy**.

| | Copy |
|---|---|
| **Before** | Start for Free |
| **After** | Organize My Thoughts for Free |

Just a few words of difference. But the message is completely different.

- **"Start"** â†’ Feels like a commitment, heavy
- **"Organize My Thoughts"** â†’ A light action you can just try, with clear value

The hypothesis is simple: **if you can feel what you'll get right from the button, you'll click it.**

We don't know yet if this change is the right answer.
